Histopatology

Histopathology is a microscopic examination of a tissue in order to study the manifestations of disease. With this exam doctors are able to classify tumours cells and lymphoid tissues. Histopathological examination of tissues starts with surgery, biopsy, or autopsy. The tissue is removed from the body and then prepared for viewing under a microscope using either chemical fixation or frozen section

Retina of a Rabbit

High-resolution electron microscopy imaging techniques enable neuroscientists to image tissue at a fine enough detail to reconstruct neuron connections in the retina. Gaining a deeper understanding of these connections can lead to new treatments for vision disorders

The Digital Michelangelo Project

A group from Stanford University built a digital model of the the "David" (by Michelangelo) by scanning the original statue in Florence using a laser scanner and assembling the resulting range images. The scan was so accurate that we can see even the finer details. Curious fact: when the original statue was still placed outside in the square, someone carved his own initials somewhere on the statue, can you find those initials?
